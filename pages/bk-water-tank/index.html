BK's Water Tank

A simulated 20 L water tank fed by a warm and a cold source, live auto-mixed to hit a target temperature and watched as it settles over time. If the target is hotter than the warm source alone can supply, an inline heater tops up the inflow — see the readouts and the "how it works" section below for the details.

How it works

Each tick, the warm/cold split ratio r is chosen to hit the target average temperature Ttarget, clamped to what's physically possible from the two sources:

r = clamp( (Ttarget − Tcold) / (Twarm − Tcold), 0, 1 )

That ratio gives the blended inflow temperature before any heating:

Tin = r · Twarm + (1 − r) · Tcold

If the target exceeds the warm source outright (r would need to exceed 1), the mix switches to 100% warm and a capped heater tops up the inflow the rest of the way:

Pneeded = ṁ · c · (Ttarget − Twarm) ,  ṁ = Q · ρ / 60
P = min(Pneeded, Pmax) ,  Tin = Twarm + P / (ṁ · c)

where Q is the flow rate (L/min), ρ is water density (1 kg/L), c is water's specific heat (4186 J/(kg·K)), and Pmax is the heater's 3 kW cap. If Pneeded exceeds the cap, the target isn't fully reachable and the warning above appears.

The container itself is a well-mixed tank of fixed volume V (20 L): incoming inflow at Tin continuously dilutes the current contents, so the container temperature settles toward Tin exponentially rather than snapping to it:

dTcontainer/dt = (Q / V) · (Tin − Tcontainer)
Tcontainer(t+Δt) = Tin + (Tcontainer(t) − Tin) · e−(Q/V)Δt

The simulation clock runs 20× real time so this settling is visible in seconds instead of minutes.
